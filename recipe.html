Japanese Fried Chicken with Slow-Roasted Crispy Pork Belly and Cauliflower Salad

The following recipes have been consolidated: - Japanese Fried Chicken - Slow-Roasted Crispy Pork Belly - Yotam Ottolenghi’s Cauliflower Salad
Prep Time: 30 minutes
Cook Time: 3 hours 30 minutes

Ingredients

  • 600 gram chicken thighs
  • 3 tablespoon light soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoon sake
  • 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  • 0.5 teaspoon sugar
  • 0.75 cup potato flour
  • 2 liter vegetable oil
  • 1000 gram pork belly
  • 1.5 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 0.25 teaspoon black pepper
  • 650 gram cauliflower
  • 0.5 teaspoon salt
  • 0.5 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 slice celery
  • 1/3 cup hazelnuts
  • 1/3 cup pomegranate seeds
  • 1 cup parsley leaves
  • 0.33 teaspoon cinnamon powder
  • 0.33 teaspoon allspice
  • 1 tablespoon sherry vinegar
  • 1.5 teaspoon maple syrup

Instructions

Preparation

Cut and marinate chicken - Cut chicken into 5cm pieces and marinate with soy sauce, sake, ginger juice, and sugar for 10 minutes.
Coat and rest chicken - Coat chicken in flour and let it rest for 5 minutes before frying.
Dry skin overnight - Pat pork belly skin dry and leave uncovered in the fridge overnight.

Cook

Preheat oven - Set oven to 140°C/285°F.
Deep fry chicken - Heat oil to 180°C and fry chicken in batches: fry for 1 minute, rest for 30 seconds, fry for 30 seconds, rest for 30 seconds, and finally fry for 30 seconds to 1 minute.
Season flesh - Drizzle flesh of pork belly with oil, sprinkle with salt, pepper, and fennel powder.
Wrap in foil - Seal pork belly in foil and roast for 2 1/2 hours.
Roast cauliflower - Toss cauliflower with oil, salt, and pepper; roast for 25-30 minutes.
Increase heat - Increase oven temperature to 240°C/465°F.
Toast hazelnuts - Roast hazelnuts for 5 minutes at 160°C/320°F, cool slightly, and chop.
30 minutes on high heat - Roast pork belly for 30 minutes until crispy.

Serve

Serve - Serve chicken with lemon wedge and Japanese mayonnaise.
Prepare salad - Combine hazelnuts, parsley, and celery; drizzle with olive oil, maple syrup, and vinegar.
Plate up - Adjust seasoning; serve salad at room temperature.
